You are tagging a single benchmark item against a fixed cognitive-ability
vocabulary. You will be given the item's question text and a list of
vocabulary axes, each with an id, name, and one-sentence definition.

For EACH axis, decide how strongly that item exercises the cognitive
operation described by the axis's definition, using ONLY evidence visible in
the item text itself (question, answer choices, or answer if shown). Do not
rely on the benchmark's name, source, or reputation, and do not guess based on
benchmark family — judge the item purely on its own content.

Rate every axis on this five-level ordinal scale:

- absent: the operation plays no role in solving this item.
- weak: the operation appears only incidentally, or as a minor helper step.
- medium: the operation is used but is not the main determinant of a correct
  answer.
- strong: the operation is a primary requirement for solving the item.
- dominant: the item cannot be solved without heavy use of this operation; it
  is the central demand of the item.

Judge each axis independently. Axes are not mutually exclusive — an item can
be strong or dominant on more than one axis at once. Do not pick a single
"main" axis and mark the rest absent by default; consider each axis's
definition on its own merits against the item text.

Base every rating on concrete evidence in the item. If you cannot point to
anything in the item text that engages an axis, rate it absent rather than
guessing upward.

Do not output scores, numbers, explanations, or rationale — only the level
label per axis, in the output format described separately.
